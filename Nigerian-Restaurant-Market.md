# Nigerian-Restaurant-Market
The Nigerian Restaurant Market
 was valued at USD 9.6 billion in 2023 and is projected to reach USD 18.4 billion by 2032, growing at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 7.9% during 2024–2032. In 2022, the market stood at USD 8.9 billion, reflecting a 7.8% year-over-year (YoY) growth in 2023. Rising urbanization, which reached 54.3% of Nigeria’s population in 2023, continues to accelerate demand for organized food service outlets.

Between 2018 and 2023, the Nigerian Restaurant Market expanded from USD 6.1 billion to USD 9.6 billion, marking a 57.4% cumulative growth over five years. The sector rebounded strongly post-pandemic, recording 11.2% YoY growth in 2021 after a 6.5% contraction in 2020 due to mobility restrictions and reduced consumer spending.

## Historical Growth and Year-over-Year Performance

In 2019, the Nigerian Restaurant Market reached USD 7.4 billion, up 8.3% from 2018. However, 2020 revenues declined to USD 6.9 billion, reflecting COVID-19-related disruptions. By 2021, the market recovered to USD 7.7 billion, representing an 11.6% YoY increase. Growth stabilized at 7.4% in 2022 and 7.8% in 2023, supported by increased consumer footfall and digital ordering.

Restaurant outlet density increased from 14.2 outlets per 100,000 population in 2018 to 19.6 outlets in 2023, a 38% rise. Organized chain restaurants accounted for 28% of total revenues in 2023, up from 19% in 2018, indicating growing formalization.

## Consumer Spending and Demographic Trends

Nigeria’s middle-class population expanded by 4.5% annually between 2017 and 2023, reaching approximately 23 million households. Average annual dining expenditure per urban household increased from USD 420 in 2018 to USD 615 in 2023, representing a 46% increase.

Quick service restaurants (QSRs) dominate with 42% market share in 2023, followed by casual dining at 34%, and fine dining at 9%. Street food and independent eateries represent 15% of total revenue, down from 21% in 2018, reflecting gradual migration toward structured formats.

Online food delivery penetration rose from 5% of total restaurant sales in 2019 to 17% in 2023, equating to USD 1.63 billion in digital sales. Mobile payment adoption in restaurant transactions climbed to 68% in 2023, compared to 39% in 2018.

## Regional Market Breakdown

South-West Nigeria, including Lagos, accounts for 48% of total Nigerian Restaurant Market revenue, generating approximately USD 4.6 billion in 2023. The South-South region contributes 18%, while the North-Central region holds 14%. The North-East and North-West collectively represent 20%, though these regions are projected to grow at a faster 9.2% CAGR through 2032 due to infrastructure investments.

Lagos alone hosts over 6,800 registered restaurant outlets, representing 31% of national outlet count. Abuja follows with 1,950 outlets, capturing 9% of total revenue.

## Investment and Infrastructure Expansion

Foreign direct investment (FDI) in Nigeria’s hospitality and food service sector reached USD 410 million in 2023, up 12% from 2022. Government-backed SME financing programs allocated approximately USD 250 million in 2023 to support small restaurant businesses and food vendors.

Commercial real estate expansion has also fueled the Nigerian Restaurant Market. Shopping mall retail space grew from 850,000 square meters in 2018 to 1.34 million square meters in 2023, a 57% increase. Nearly 36% of new retail space developed between 2021 and 2023 included dedicated restaurant and food court facilities.

## Employment and Economic Contribution

The Nigerian Restaurant Market employed approximately 1.42 million people in 2023, up from 1.05 million in 2018, reflecting a 35% increase in five years. The sector contributes an estimated 1.8% to Nigeria’s GDP, compared to 1.3% in 2017.

Average monthly wages in organized restaurant chains increased from USD 120 in 2018 to USD 185 in 2023, representing a 54% wage growth. Labor accounts for 29% of total operating costs, while food supplies represent 41%, utilities 12%, and rent 18%.

## Menu Trends and Pricing Dynamics

Local cuisine dominates menus, with Nigerian traditional dishes accounting for 63% of total sales in 2023. International cuisine, including American and Asian food formats, represents 37%, up from 29% in 2018.

Average meal prices increased by 22% between 2021 and 2023, largely due to inflation, which averaged 18.6% annually during this period. Despite price hikes, customer footfall increased by 9% in 2023, indicating resilient demand.

Plant-based menu offerings grew by 14% annually since 2020, now appearing in 28% of urban restaurant menus. Beverage sales account for 21% of total revenue, while desserts contribute 11%.

## Competitive Landscape and Market Concentration

The top 10 organized restaurant chains collectively control 32% of total market revenue, up from 24% in 2018. Franchise-based outlets grew at a 10.4% CAGR between 2019 and 2023, faster than independent restaurants at 5.9% CAGR.

Average annual revenue per organized outlet reached USD 485,000 in 2023, compared to USD 310,000 in 2018, reflecting improved operational efficiency and digital integration.

Delivery-only “cloud kitchen” models expanded rapidly, increasing from 120 units in 2020 to 540 units in 2023, a 350% growth in three years.

## Future Projections and Forecasts to 2032

The Nigerian Restaurant Market is forecast to reach USD 11.1 billion in 2025, USD 13.8 billion by 2028, and USD 18.4 billion by 2032. Annual growth rates are projected to remain above 7.5% through 2030, supported by urban population growth expected to hit 60% by 2030.

Digital ordering is expected to account for 28% of total sales by 2030, representing nearly USD 4.5 billion in transaction value. Outlet count is projected to surpass 32,000 nationwide by 2032, up from approximately 21,500 in 2023.

Per capita annual restaurant spending is forecast to increase from USD 44 in 2023 to USD 76 by 2032, a 72% rise over nine years.

## Data-Backed Conclusion

The Nigerian Restaurant Market has demonstrated robust resilience, growing from USD 6.1 billion in 2018 to USD 9.6 billion in 2023, and is set to nearly double to USD 18.4 billion by 2032 at a 7.9% CAGR. With 42% QSR dominance, 17% digital sales penetration, 1.42 million employees, and 48% revenue concentration in South-West Nigeria, the sector shows strong structural expansion.

Rising urbanization, expanding retail infrastructure, and digital payment adoption at 68% transaction share will continue to reshape the Nigerian Restaurant Market. By 2032, organized formats are expected to exceed 40% market share, cementing the industry’s transformation into a structured, high-growth food service ecosystem.
